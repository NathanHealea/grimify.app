## Summary

Comprehensive catalog of Green Stuff World (GSW) paint ranges extracted from the official 2025 color chart PDF. Covers Primers (7 products, 60ml), Acrylic Colors (104 MAXX-formula paints, 17ml), and Fluor Paints (10 fluorescent colors, 17ml). GSW uses 4-digit reference numbers as identifiers. All reference numbers and paint names below are estimated from the PDF color chart and may contain minor transcription errors due to small print size.

## Brand Metadata Notes

GSW paints use 4-digit reference numbers as their primary identifier. The numbering does not follow a strict sequential pattern — older products use lower numbers while newer additions jump to the 3xxx range.

| Product Line | Ref Range | Paint Types | Count |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Primers | 1742–3510 | Matt, Gloss | 7 |
| Acrylic Colors (MAXX) | 0715–3259 | Standard | 104 |
| Fluor Paints | 1070–1799 | Fluorescent | 10 |
| **Total (ingested)** | | | **121** |

### Key Differences from Other Brands

- **4-digit reference numbers:** GSW uses simple 4-digit product codes (e.g., 1776, 3207) rather than dotted notation like Vallejo (70.xxx) or named-only systems like Citadel and Army Painter.
- **MAXX formula:** The main acrylic line uses GSW's proprietary MAXX formula, marketed as high-pigment, self-leveling paints in dropper bottles.
- **No triad/workflow system:** Unlike Army Painter (Flexible Triad) or Vallejo (BSL system), GSW does not publish a formal shadow/base/highlight system. Colors are presented on the chart organized by hue.
- **Extensive specialty lines:** GSW has an unusually large number of specialty product lines (colorshift, chrome, UV resin, liquid pigments, dipping inks) compared to other miniature paint brands.
- **Fluorescent as separate line:** Fluor Paints are a distinct product line from the standard acrylic colors, using fluorescent pigments for UV-reactive effects.

### Transcription Notes

All reference numbers and paint names were extracted visually from the PDF color chart. The small print size means some entries may have minor transcription errors, particularly:
- Digits that look similar at small sizes (3/8, 5/6, 1/7)
- Creative paint names that are unusual or hard to parse (e.g., "Bluegrotto", "Gungrove", "Isverlan Blue")
- The Dark Browns and some Fluor Paint reference numbers have lower confidence due to image resolution

Cross-referencing against GSW's online product catalog is recommended to verify entries.
